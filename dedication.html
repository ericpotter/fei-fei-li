  • Caltech 101

    The predecessor of ImageNet

    Caltech 101 is a commonly used dataset for image classification research, consisting of images of 101 different categories of objects.
    About 40 to 800 images per category. Most categories have about 50 images. Collected in September 2003 by researchers(Fei-Fei Li,
    Marco Andreetto, and Marc'Aurelio Ranzato) and students at the California Institute of Technology (Caltech). The size of each image
    is roughly 300 x 200 pixels.

    This dataset serves as an ideal platform for developing and testing classification algorithms, allowing researchers to train machine learning
    models to differentiate and recognize different object categories. What sets Caltech 101 apart is its inclusion of multiple categories
    while maintaining a relatively modest scale, making it a popular experimental dataset for evaluating the performance and generalization
    of classification algorithms.

    Caltech 101, as one of the early image classification datasets, sparked interest and demand among researchers to create larger and more
    diverse datasets. The creation of ImageNet can be seen as an expansion and evolution from datasets containing a few hundred categories to datasets
    encompassing tens of thousands of categories.

  • ImageNet

    Cornerstone of modern computer vision research

    ImageNet is a large-scale image database and a pivotal platform for visual recognition research. It houses over 14 million images
    spanning more than 20,000 categories, making it one of the most extensive datasets in the field of computer vision. Developed
    initially by Dr. Fei-Fei Li research Team at Stanford University, ImageNet serves as a benchmark dataset for training and evaluating
    image classification and object detection models.

    ImageNet gained significant attention with the introduction of the ImageNet Large Scale Visual Recognition Challenge (ILSVRC) in 2010.
    This challenge aimed to evaluate algorithms for object detection and image classification tasks using the ImageNet dataset. The ILSVRC
    spurred rapid progress in the development of deep learning models, particularly convolutional neural networks (CNNs), which demonstrated
    remarkable performance improvements in image recognition tasks.

    Beyond research, ImageNet has also been pivotal in raising awareness about the capabilities and limitations of artificial intelligence.
    It has highlighted the challenges of bias and fairness in AI, emphasizing the importance of diverse and representative datasets.

  • AI4ALL

    Changemakers in AI

    AI4ALL is an educational initiative founded by Dr. Fei-Fei Li that aims to promote diversity and inclusion in the field of AI and
    computer science. The program provides opportunities for high school students from underrepresented backgrounds, including women
    and minorities, to explore AI and related fields through hands-on learning experiences.

    The goal of AI4ALL is to inspire and empower the next generation of diverse AI leaders by providing access to resources and mentorship.
    Beyond the summer programs, AI4ALL supports alumni with ongoing educational and professional development opportunities, fostering
    a supportive community that encourages continued engagement in AI and tech.

    AI4ALL plays a crucial role in addressing diversity gaps in AI by equipping students from diverse backgrounds with the skills, knowledge,
    and confidence to pursue careers and contribute meaningfully to the field. Through its initiatives, AI4ALL strives to create a more inclusive
    and equitable future for AI innovation.

    "Illuminating the dark spaces of healthcare with ambient intelligence"

    This is a paper published in Nature, and according to Fei-Fei Li, it took 8 years to complete. The research team behind this paper included over
    a dozen Stanford undergraduates, graduates, and postdoctoral researchers, along with nearly ten doctors and professors from the same medical school.
    The paper cites and summarizes over a dozen AI papers and half a dozen medical journal articles.
